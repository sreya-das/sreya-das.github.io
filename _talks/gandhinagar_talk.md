Abstract:
- Quantum control is a very new class of problem of bilinear systems evolving in Lie groups like SO(n) and SU(n). In quantum control, the same control is used simultaneously to highly dispersive parameters to control an infinite number of objects. In Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR) Spectrometer, such bilinear control excites the system over a broader chemical shift region with the exact resonance frequency. Constant control does not work for controlling the magnetic moment of the nuclear magnetic spin. An oscillatory control of exact frequency is needed. We will learn the mechanism of chirp excitation followed by a brief description of the CHORUS pulse sequence as an example of broadband excitation of NMR spectrometer control.
