My research focuses on quantum optimal control, pulse engineering of magnetic resonance, and control theory, with an emphasis on developing robust and physically realizable control strategies for complex quantum dynamical systems. My current research direction is very much relevant to emerging areas such as quantum information processing, quantum sensing, and advanced magnetic resonance technologies. I currently work on designing robust optimal control pulses for parahydrogen-induced polarization transfer (PHIP), addressing realistic experimental imperfections such as state-dependent and state-independent interactions.  My current work is part of the QuE-MRT project in collaboration with NVISION, where optimized control pulses are being integrated into the POLARIS PHIP polarizer to generate hyperpolarized carbon-13 agents for enhanced MRI imaging. I have also expertise in designing chirp and composite pulse for NMR and developed methods in classical control theory for efficient pole placement, connecting quantum spin physics with modern control and optimization frameworks.
