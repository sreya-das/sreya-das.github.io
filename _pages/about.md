My research focuses on **quantum optimal control, pulse engineering in magnetic resonance, and control theory**, with an emphasis on developing **robust and physically realizable control strategies for complex quantum dynamical systems**. These research directions are closely connected to emerging areas such as **quantum information processing, quantum sensing, and advanced magnetic resonance technologies**.

Currently, I work on designing **robust optimal control pulses for parahydrogen-induced polarization transfer (PHIP)**, addressing realistic experimental imperfections including **state-dependent and state-independent interactions**. This work is part of the **QuE-MRT project in collaboration with NVISION Imaging Technologies GmbH** and **University of Ulm**, where optimized control pulses are being integrated into the **POLARIS PHIP polarizer** to generate **hyperpolarized carbon-13 agents for enhanced MRI imaging**. In addition, I have worked on developing **chirp and composite pulse sequences for excitation of quantum systems**, which were experimentally implemented in **NMR systems**.

My educational background is rooted in **control systems and control theory**, which has provided me with strong foundations in the analysis and design of classical control methodologies. I have experience in designing controllers such as **PI and PID controllers for physical systems**, including approaches based on **efficient pole-placement methods**. Overall, my research bridges **classical and quantum control**, with the goal of advancing **robust control techniques for both fundamental quantum systems and practical technological applications**.
